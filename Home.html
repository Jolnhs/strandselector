Julia Ortiz National High School

TVL (Technical-Vocational-Livelihood)

The TVL strand focuses on providing students with technical and vocational skills that prepare them for various livelihood opportunities. Students in this strand engage in hands-on training and practical experiences related to specific trades or industries such as culinary arts, automotive, electronics, and more.

TVL equips students with practical skills that are highly relevant to the workforce, allowing them to pursue careers in specific technical fields.

STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics)

STEM is a specialized strand that focuses on developing students' skills and knowledge in the fields of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics. Students in STEM engage in advanced coursework, experiments, and projects that prepare them for careers in science and technology-related fields.

STEM education emphasizes critical thinking, problem-solving, and creativity, making students well-equipped for higher education and STEM-related professions.

HUMSS (Humanities and Social Sciences)

HUMSS is a strand that focuses on the study of human society, culture, and individual behavior. Students in HUMSS explore subjects such as history, philosophy, literature, psychology, and sociology.

HUMSS provides a foundation for students interested in pursuing careers in social sciences, humanities, law, journalism, and other fields that require a deep understanding of human society.

SMAW (Shielded Metal Arc Welding)

Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW) is a welding process that uses a flux-coated electrode to create an electric arc for joining metals. It is commonly used in construction, fabrication, and maintenance work. SMAW is known for its versatility and ability to work in various positions, making it suitable for different welding applications.

Students in the SMAW strand learn essential welding skills, safety procedures, and gain hands-on experience in creating welded joints using different types of metals.
